#+TITLE: Vinyl Siding

* Filling small holes

- Remove the burrs with a box cutter or putty knife
- Degloss the area around the hole with 220 grit sandpaper
- Wipe the surface clean of dust
- Apply patching material
- Smooth the filler level with the siding using a wet putty knife
- Allow the filler to cure
- Smooth the repair with 220 grit sandpaper
- Clean the surface
- Apply primer and paint

* Siding panel repair

- Measure dimensions of existing panels
- If you need to paint the siding, buy a dark color --- painting a light colored siding dark may cause it to warp in direct sunlight
- How siding is installed
  - Sheathing is attached to studs
  - A weather barrier (Tyvek) is attached to the outside of the sheathing
  - Vinyl siding components are installed over the barrier
    - Starter strip (at the bottom)
    - J-channels and sill channels along windows and doors
    - Corner pieces on building corners
    - Siding panels fit within channels of trim
    - Each row of siding panels locks into the row below it
    - Secured to the house with nails
- So to replace a panel
  - Expose the nailing fin that's at the top of the panel
    - Find a seam on the panel that's above the panel you wish to replace
    - Pull up on the seam, exposing the bottom of the channel
    - Swipe your hand across the bottom of the upper panel gently, separating the two panels
    - Can also use a vinyl unlocking tool to separate panels
  - Remove nails on nailing fin at the top of the panel you wish to replace
  - Lightly tap on the damaged panel to remove it
  - Repair damage to the weather barrier
    - Patch holes with Tyvek tape
    - Replace larger damaged sections
  - Use the old piece to measure out how much new vinyl you need to cut
    - Use a speed square to make sure that your cutting lines are straight
  - Cut the end that will be hidden in the channel or behind the seam with vinyl or tin snips
  - If necessary, cut tabs on the top and bottom to allow for expansion and contraction to replicate the factory hem and bottom lock
  - To install the new panel
    - Pull up the adjacent a bit to create some slack
    - Push the panel up into place and hear it lock in
    - Reattach the adjacent panel
    - Panels must overlap at least 1" but must have room to slide side to side to accomodate expansion and contraction
    - Add siding nails every 16" on the top
    - Leave the nail heads about 1/16th inch above the nail hem, in order to allow the siding to slide a bit --- this prevents buckling and warping
    - Pull down the upper piece a bit with the siding removal tool and lock it into the lower piece

* Installing siding around windows

- Ensure the window itself is in good condition
- Put flashing around the window
