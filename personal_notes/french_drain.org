* Instructions
- Dig a trench approximately 18 inches deep and 9 - 12 inches wide
- Trench should slope downwards at least 2 inches for every 10 feet of length
- Will probably need to rent a backhoe/excavator to dig the trench
- Line the trench with water-permeable filter fabric
- Leave the filter fabric open with at least 10 inches of excess filter fabric along the sides
- Pour and compact at least 3 inches of gravel bedding or landscaping stone on top of the filter fabric
- Install inlet grate at the top of the trench
- Lay pipe in the trench, making sure that the perforations are pointed downwards
- Test water flow by pouring water into the top of the pipe and verifying that it comes out the bottom
- Cover the pipe with approximately another 3 inches of gravel or crushed stone --- however make sure that the top of the gravel remains below ground level
- Wrap the excess filter fabric over the top of the gravel covered pipe for additional protection
- Backfill the remainder of the trench with topsoil and compact
- When backfilling, cover the top of the inlet grate with some excess filter fabric to ensure that dirt and stones don't fall into the drain
- Reseed the surface with grass or cover with landscaping stone as desired

* Alternative Instructions
- Use [[https://www.ndspro.com/us/en/products/drainage/french-drain-systems/ez-flow-french-drains][NDS EZFlow]] prefabricated french drain sections
- Same instructions as above, but skip the gravel steps, as the prefab drain sections are wrapped with polystyrene pellets that mimic gravel

* Digging a sloped trench
- Put in stakes and stretch mason's string between them to mark out the trench
- Adjust the string so that it's level
- Divide the trench into segments, and set the depth for each segment
- Dig each segment of the trench
- Then use some of the leftover dirt to smooth out the steps between each segment
