#+TITLE: Fountain Pens

* Tips
- Use distilled water
- If the pen is very dirty, try a 10:1 dilution ammonia
- Do not use ammonia on aluminum, brass or copper, as it can lead to corrosion
- Do not use hot water --- could lead to warping

* Basic Cleaning

When refilling with the same ink:

1. Uncap the fountain pen and unscrew the nib section
2. Remove cartridge or converter
3. Hold the grip section under cool running water for a few seconds
4. Soak the grip section in a cup of water
5. Replace the water if it gets saturated with ink
6. Remove the nib section from the cup and let it dry for 24 hours
7. Once the nib is dry, load a new cartridge and screw the grip section back into the barrel

* Full Flush

When changing inks or if you suspect the nib is dirty
1. If possible disassemble and rinse the grip section, following step 3 from above (if not possible, skip to step 5)
2. Place the open end of the converter or barrel in a cup of clean water
3. Flush the barrel/converter by filling and emptying the ink chamber until it's clean, i.e. until it's visibly clean or until the water coming out runs clear
4. Reattach the barrel or converter to the grip section
5. Use the converter or built-in filling system to flush the feed
6. Keep flushing until the water runs clear
