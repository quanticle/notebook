* Tiny Cars

** Nissan Versa
- Blind spot monitor available at the SV trim level
- MSRP: $19,970
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/nissan/versa/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/nissan/versa][Car and Driver review]]

* Small Cars

** Toyota Corolla
- Blind-spot monitor with rear-cross traffic alert optional
- MSRP: $24,500
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/toyota/corolla/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/toyota/corolla][Car and Driver review]]
  - Good fuel economy
  - Small trunk
  - Infotainment system can be laggy

** Honda Civic
- Blind-spot monitor only available on the EX trim level
- MSRP: $27,000
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/honda/civic/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/honda/civic-2024][Car and Driver review]]
  - This appears to be the reviewers' favorite small car
  - However, it's also the most expensive small car
  - Fuel economy isn't great either

** Hyundai Elantra
- Comes with blind-spot monitor standard
- MSRP: $21,625
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/hyundai/elantra/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/hyundai/elantra][Car and Driver review]]
  - Reviews highlight the 10-year/100,000 mile powertrain warranty
  - Spacious rear seats
  - Also seems to get really good fuel economy, while still delivering decent power
  - Good looking car

** Kia Forte
- Blind spot monitor available on the LXS trim level, with the LXS technology package
- MSRP: $22,145
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/kia/forte/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/kia/forte][Car and Driver review]]
  - Same warranty as the Hyundai Elantra, but doesn't include roadside assistance
  - Similar fuel economy as the Elantra as well
  - Large trunk

** Nissan Sentra
- Blind spot monitor standard
- MSRP: $21,180
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/nissan/sentra/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/nissan/sentra][Car and Driver review]]
  - Slow
  - Not especially good fuel economy
  - Cheap, but not that cheap compared with something like the Hyundai Elantra

** Subaru Impreza
- Blind spot monitor available as an option on the Sport trim level
- MSRP: $26,895
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/subaru/impreza/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/subaru/impreza][Car and Driver review]]
  - Four-wheel drive standard
  - Good handling, but acceleration is a bit slow
  - Smooth ride
  - Doesn't have A/C vents for the rear passengers

** Mazda 3
- Blind spot monitor standard
- MSRP: $24,170
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/mazda/3/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/mazda/mazda-3][Car and Driver review]]
  - The best looking and best driving small car
  - Don't get the hatchback version --- terrible rear visibility
  - Infotainment system is controlled by a knob, rather than being a touchscreen like on every other car

* Small SUVs

** Honda HR-V
- Blind spot monitor available on the Sport trim level
- MSRP: $27,200
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/honda/hr-v/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/honda/hr-v-2024][Car and Driver review]]
  - Underpowered engine
  - Uncomfortable front seats
  - No USB charge ports for rear-seat passengers
  - Roomy front seats, but rear seats are somewhat cramped

** Hyundai Venue
- Blind-spot monitor only available on the SEL trim level
- MSRP: $22,000
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/hyundai/venue/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/hyundai/venue][Car and Driver review]]
  - No all-wheel drive option
  - Not great fuel economy
  - Roomy interior that's easy to get in and out of

** Hyundai Kona
- Comes with blind-spot monitor standard
- MSRP: $24,250
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/hyundai/kona/2025/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/hyundai/kona][Car and Driver review]]
  - Seems like a solid small SUV
  - Lots of legroom in the interior
  - Soft suspension means that it doesn't handle very well and can lead to excess bouncing over bumps
  - Reviewers like the cargo cover that can fold out of the way for larger items

** Kia Seltos
- Blind spot monitoring available on the S trim level
- MSRP: $25,090
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/kia/seltos/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/kia/seltos][Car and Driver review]]
  - Good styling, with Land Rover-esque design touches
  - Lots of cargo space
  - Comfortable seats

** Kia Niro
- Only comes as a hybrid, plug-in hybrid or EV
- Blind-spot monitoring standard
- MSRP: $26,940
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/kia/niro/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/kia/niro][Car and Driver review]]
  - Impressive fuel economy, up to 53 mpg, as befitting a hybrid
  - Quiet interior
  - Reviewers call it out for having USB-A charging ports instead of USB-C

** Nissan Kicks
- Blind spot monitor standard
- MSRP: $21,340
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/nissan/kicks/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/nissan/kicks-2024][Car and Driver review]]
  - Slow engine
  - No all-wheel drive option
  - Roomy rear seats
  - However, it's low price means it's a good value
  - /Note: this information pertains to the 2024 Kicks. It has been significantly redesigned for 2025, with an upgraded engine, larger body, and much different styling/

** Toyota Corolla Cross
- Blind spot monitor only available on the LE trim level
- MSRP: $26,190
- Reviews
  - [[https://www.edmunds.com/toyota/corolla-cross/][Edmunds review]]
  - [[https://www.caranddriver.com/toyota/corolla-cross][Car and Driver review]]
  - De-facto replacement for the Toyota CH-R
  - Not great legroom in the rear --- compares poorly even against smaller competitors such as the Hyundai Kona
  - Slow engine
  - Comfortable ride
