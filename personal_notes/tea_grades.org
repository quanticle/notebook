* Tea Grades

** Whole Leaf
- OP - Orange Pekoe: note that the name has nothing to do with the flavor, but rather refers to the orange color of tea
- OP1 - Orange pekoe with a more delicate flavor
- OPA - Orange pekoe with a bolder flavor
- OPS - (Indonesia) Orange pekoe superior
- FOP - Flowery Orange Pekoe
  - Next step up from OP
  - 2nd grade in Assam, Dooars and Bangladesh
  - FOP1 is the highest subgrade of FOP
- GFOP - Golden Flowery Orange Pekoe
  - Top grade in Milima and Marinyn (Kenya)
  - Uncommon in Assam and Darjeeling
- TGFOP - Tippy Golden Flowery Orange Pekoe
  - Primary grade of Assam and Darjeeling
- FTGFOP - Finest Tippy Golden Flowery Orange Pekoe
  - Highest grade of tea
  - Subcategories:
    - FTGFOP1
    - STGFOP
    - SFTGFOP

** Broken Leaf
- BP - Broken Pekoe (basic grade, corresponds to OP on the whole leaf side)
  - Common origins: Indonesia, Assam, Sri Lanka, South India
- FP - Flowery Pekoe
  - Not to be confused with /FOP/, which is a whole leaf grade
  - Usually found in Assam and South India
- BOP - Broken Orange Pekoe
  - Mid-grade broken leaf tea
  - Sources: Assam, Sri Lanka, Southern India, Java, China
  - FBOP - Flowery Broken Orange Pekoe
    - Highest subgrade of BOP
    - Usually found in Sri Lanka
- GBOP - Golden Broken Orange Pekoe
  - Second highest grade of broken leaf tea
  - Mainly from Sri Lanka
- TGFBOP - Tippy Golden Flowery Broken Orange Pekoe
  - Highest grade of broken leaf tea
