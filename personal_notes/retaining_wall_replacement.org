* Process
 
- Calculate trench dimensions
  - Trench needs to be wide enough to accomodate the first layer of the wall (between 10" and 20", depending on wall design)
  - 6" deep
  - Plus another inch of depth for every foot of wall height
- Draw the curve or radius for the wall on the ground with paint
- Dig the trench
  - Use a pointed shovel to dig down
  - Use a rake to smooth dirt
  - Make sure that base is approximately level
  - Tamp dirt with hand tamper
  - Lay 5" of gravel base
    - Lay 2" of gravel
    - Tamp
    - Verify that gravel is level
    - Repeat
  - Lay 1" leveling sand
    - Place PVC pipe on gravel
    - Pour sand and use the 2 x 4 to scree the sand flat, so it just covers the pipe
    - Do not tamp
    - Remove the pipe, fill in the gap with more sand and scree it flat again
- Build the wall by stacking blocks in alternating rows
  - Pay extra attention when building the first row
  - Use level to ensure that the blocks are level
  - Use a mallet to make adjustments if the blocks are not level
- After each layer of the wall is dry-fit, secure it to the layer below with masonry adhesive
- At the end, add a layer of cap stones, once again securing it to the layer below with masonry adhesive
