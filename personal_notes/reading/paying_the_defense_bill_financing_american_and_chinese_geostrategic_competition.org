#+TITLE: Paying The Defense Bill: Financing American and Chinese Geostrategic Competition
#+OPTIONS: toc:6; H:6

Source: https://tnsr.org/2023/04/paying-the-defense-bill-financing-american-and-chinese-geostrategic-competition/

- Geopolitical competition tends to last for decades
- Success in great-power competition requires spending that can be sustained over the long term
- The US is faced with a large bill after decades of war associated with the Global War on Terror (GWOT)
- Shifting from a counterinsurgency focus to a focus on great-power competition will add further costs
- Competition with China is but one part of US foreign policy
- Other priorities for the US include
  - Deterring Russian aggression in Europe
  - Commitments in the Middle East
  - Ongoing global counterterrorism
- /But do we actually need to fund all of these commitments? What if we, for example, cut our obligations in the Middle East?/
- The 2021 withdrawal from Afghanistan was intended to better align America's spending with its priorities, but funding is still going to fall short
- A 2021 comprehensive study indicated that the Department of Defense's budget will need to increase by 11% in real terms by 2033 in order to fund existing plans
- China is also spending heavily
  - Aims to have a "world-class" military by 2049
  - Has increased its defense budget by an average of 7% a year every year since 2012
  - However People's Liberation Army analysts also claim that China's defense spending is too low to meet its priorities
  - Defense spending is increasing faster than revenue growth → increasing proportion of the budget spent on defense
