* How to Drive A Pontoon Boat
- Trim motor so that the propeller is in the water
- Put motor into neutral
- Start ignition
- Think of throttle as more like a shift lever than an accelerator/brake
  - Forward, neutral and reverse positions
  - Can use reverse to slow down
- To pull away from the dock in reverse
  - Turn wheel all the way towards the dock
  - Give the motor a few bursts of forward to separate the rear of the boat from the dock
  - The put the boat in reverse and reverse away from the dock at an angle
- When docking
  - Try to dock with the wind, so that the wind is pushing you into the dock
  - Approach dock at a 45° angle
  - Approach at a very slow idle
  - When you reach the dock put the boat into neutral
  - Tie the front of the boat to a post or cleat
  - Turn wheel toward dock and reverse to pull the back of the boat into the dock
  - Always stop the boat with the throttle before grabbing anything outside the boat
- Feather the throttle, using forward and reverse to pivot in place
- Always stop the boat first, then figure out what to do, then start doing it
