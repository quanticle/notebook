#+TITLE: Furnace Tips

* Flame Sensor

** Symptoms
- Flames shut off immediately after ignition
- Heat shuts off before a cycle fully completes

** Possible Solutions
- Clean the flame sensor
  - Turn off power to the furnace
  - Turn off gas
  - Open the side of the furnace
  - The flame sensor is usually to the right, on the opposite side of the igniter assembly, and is held in place with a metal bracket
  - Has one wire going into it, while the igniter has two wires
  - Disconnect the wire from the flame sensor
  - Use a nut-driver or screwdriver to remove the screw or bolt holding the flame sensor in place
  - Clean off the soot with steel wool, or emery cloth, being careful not to touch the metal portion of the flame sensor with your hands
  - Reinstall the flame sensor
  - Reattach the wire
  - Put the side back on
  - Restart gas
  - Restart power
- Replace the flame sensor
  - Find a flame sensor of the same shape as the one already installed
  - Then go through through the same steps as cleaning the flame sensor, only replace the flame sensor entirely rather than just cleaning it
