#+TITLE: Drywall Repair

- To repair drywall in a manner that allows further mounting of hardware
  - Use a Sawzall or hacksaw to square off the hole in the drywall
  - Cut a section of 1 x 4" furring strip 4" longer than the hole
  - Drill a screw into the center of the furring strip to act as a handle
  - Slide the furring strip into the hole and center it
  - Drill two drywall screws on either side of the hole to attach the furring strip to the back of the drywall
  - Cut a section of drywall to the approximate size of the hole
  - Use screws to attach the drywall patch to the furring strip
  - Use spackle to fill in the cracks between the drywall patch and the pre-existing drywall
  - Sand and paint
