#+TITLE: Painting An Outdoor Metal Door

* This Old House
- Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b0QcTu-5D1I
- Remove door from hinges
- Remove hardware
- Lightly sand with 400 grit sandpaper
- Vacuum dust off door
- Wipe door with wet rag
- Use DTM (direct to metal) paint
- Use brushes to paint angled surfaces and panels
- Use foam rollers to paint rest of door
- Use long strokes with low pressure with foam roller
- Let dry for two hours, then apply a second coat
- Once the second coat is dry (after another two hours), reattach hardware and re-hang door

* Do It Yourself
- Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CGE_GtD0koU
- It is possible to paint a door while leaving it on its hinges
- Make sure to do so when there is no rain in the forecast
- Painting a door while on its hinges might take longer
- Sand one day, primer coat, then paint
- Wash door with degreasing cleaner
- Rinse surface and ensure it's dry
- Sand with a fine-grit sanding block (300-grit or higher)
- Sand the entire surface
- Remove weather stripping and kick plates
- Use painter's tape to tape off areas that shouldn't be painted
- Tape newspaper or brown paper over non-removable kick plates or windows
- Use exterior primer and exterior paint designed for application to previously painted metal
- Use a trim roller with a low-nap roller cover to apply primer and paint
- Have a brush handy to cover corners and angles
- Once the primer has dried completely, apply two coats of paint, allowing the first to dry completely before applying the second
