* Fireplace measurements
- Fireplace is a 34" circle

* Outdoor Fireplace pad
- One option is to create a concrete pad
- Can also use pavers
- Need to measure the size of the fireplace

* General concrete instructions
- Mark out the area for the pad with string line and wooden stakes
- Excavate the area to a depth of 6 inches and level the ground with sand
- Using 2x4s, build a form just inside the area you've excavated
- Mix the concrete and fill the form, making sure the edges are filled
- Using a screed (long 2x4) scrape the top of the concrete in order to level it
- Wait until the concrete has just started to set --- should be still malleable but not liquid
- Use a concrete float to smooth the surface of the concrete
- Before the concrete has set fully use a groover to create control joints in the concrete
- Wait for the concrete to cure for several days
- Apply a concrete sealer to seal the concrete and protect it from the elements

* Pavers
- Concrete seems like a lot of hassle
- 12in x 12in x 1.5in
- Will want to dig out the sod
- Then raise and level the soil with sand
- Tamp sand down to form firm and level foundation
- Place paving stones on sand to form base for outdoor fireplace

* Digging up sod
- Water the area to be dug up
- Mark out the area with line or spray paint
- Using a sharp flat shovel, cut a strip 2-3 shovel blade widths wide and 1 shovel width long
- Go to the far side of the strip and pry up the sod
- Get down on your knees and push the shovel into the cut, parallel to the soil to cut the roots
- Pry up the pad and put it aside
- Repeat until the entire area is clear

** Instructions
- Use string and spray paint to mark out pad location
- 4" of gravel mix
- 1" of sand
- Need to dig approx 7"
- Use pointed shovel to cut sod and dig down
- Use a rake to smooth the dirt
- Check that the base is level
- Tamp dirt with hand tamper
- Lay weed barrier
- Lay gravel base - 4" of compacted gravel
  - Lay 1" of gravel
  - Tamp
  - Verify that the gravel is level, adding gravel as necessary to adjust
  - Repeat
- Place PVC pipe on gravel
- Pour sand and use a long 2x4 to scree the sand flat, so it just covers the pipes (do not compact)
- Remove the pipe, fill in the gap with more sand, and scree it flat again
- Place pavers on top of sand, being careful not to step on the sand while placing the pavers;
- Spread joint locking sand on top of pavers
- Use a push broom to fill the gaps between the pavers with joint locking sand
- Spray water to activate the joint locking sand
- Let dry before using
