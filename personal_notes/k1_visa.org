#+TITLE: K1 Visa

- K1 Visa --- Fiancé visa
- Non-immigrant visa
- Permits the foreign-born fiancé of a US citizen to enter the United States to marry their partner
- US citizen needs to file form I-129F
  - Proof of citizenship of US petitioner
  - Passport details
  - Address history
  - Employment histories
  - Information about parents
    - Names
    - Places of birth
    - Dates of birth
    - Current addresses
  - Include photos with dates and locations
  - Photos of boarding passes
  - Photos with friends and family
  - Letters of intent to marry within 90 days
  - US citizen must submit proof of income --- form I-134 (Proof of financial support)
  - G-1145 --- need to fill this out to receive e-mail updates on petition
  - Can be helpful to include photocopies of both partners' passports, as that can provide further evidence of visiting each other
  - Form must be sent physically --- cannot be submitted online
  - Send via FedEx recorded delivery
  - Never send original documents
  - Make duplicates of everything you're submitting
- US citizen partner is the petitioner
- Other partner is the beneficiary
- Eligibility requirements
  - Be engaged to a US citizen
  - Have the intention of marrying within 90 days of entering the US
  - Have proof of your relationship prior to the engagement
  - Get married according to US laws while in the US on the K1 visa
  - Must have met in person at least once in the last two years
  - Previous marriages must be legally terminated
- Document requirements
  - Passport that's valid for more than six months after the intended visit to the US
  - Two recent US visa photos
  - Form DS-160 confirmation page and confirmation code
  - Interview schedule letter
  - Approved form I-129F
  - Criminal background documents --- Police Clearance Report
  - Divorce or death certificates in the case of previous marriages
  - Evidence of the relationship with the US citizen
  - Medical examination documents
  - Proof of payment of fees
- Application steps
  - US citizen must obtain permission from USCIS to sponsor their foreign citizen partner
    - File form I-129F
    - Official will go through evidence of relationship
      - Will assess intent to get married
      - Assess other eligibility criteria
    - Petition will then go to National Visa Center (NVC)
    - NVC will inform the embassy or consulate that's closest to the foreign partner
  - Once this permission has been granted, then the foreign partner must apply for the K1 visa at an embassy or consulate abroad
    - Fill out form DS-160 --- nonimmigrant visa application
      - Personal information
      - Why you plan to go to the US
      - After submitting, keep the confirmation page and confirmation code
    - Schedule the visa interview
    - Complete medical examinations
      - Must be completed by a licensed physician
      - Report must contain
        - Current health conditions
        - US-required vaccinations
    - Compile documents
      - Ask immigration lawyer about required K-1 visa documents
      - Similar to documents that were submitted by the US-citizen fiancé to get the visa application permission
    - Attend visa interview
      - Have documents ready
      - Be prepared to answer questions about the relationship
        - Fiancé's full name
        - Date of birth
        - Where fiancé was born
        - Current residence
        - Fiancé's profession
        - Fiancé's previous marriages and relationships
        - Has fiancé ever visited you or have you ever visited them, along with details of the visits
        - Where did engagement happen
        - Was there an engagement party
      - Personal information, such as name, DOB, location of birth, etc.
      - Have you ever been in the US before
      - What are your plans once in the US
      - Criminal background questions
      - Profession
      - The interview is very important, and will make the difference as to whether the K-1 is approved or not
- Cannot obtain K-1 visa without an approved I-129F
- K1 visa application rejections
  - Criminal activity
  - Overstaying in the US on a previous visa
- Processing time will vary depending on multiple factors
  - USCIS processing time
  - How busy the foreign consulate or embassy is
  - Will take multiple months, and delays are likely
  - Delays are the result of partial or full closures of immigration offices, high caseloads, further investigation, and other administrative reasons
  - Processing time is at least 6 months, and can take up to 9-12 months routinely
