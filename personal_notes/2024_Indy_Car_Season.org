- 85 laps
- On lap 1 Will Power jumps Felix Rosenqvist for the lead
- Lap 4 --- Alexander Rossi pulls into pit lane with a potential problem --- had a flat tire caused by his teammate, Pato O'Ward running into him
- Lap 6 --- Rosenqvist is falling back through the order, having been passed by Power, Newgarden, Ericsson, and Herta
- Lap 11 --- Pato O'Ward has to serve a drive-through penalty for avoidable contact on his teammate, Alexander Rossi
- Lap 15 --- Christian Rasmussen crashes, clips Jack Harvey, who has sidepod damage as a result, full course yellow declared
- Will Power stops under the full course yellow and a few other cars come in as well, but the majority stay out
- Power drops to 12th as a result, and Josef Newgarden is in the lead
- Scott Dixon stops on the same lap as Will Power
- Lap 24 --- Scott Dixon passes Will Power for 12th, meaning that effectively he has the lead over Power
- Lap 27 --- Christian Lundgaard assessed a 5 position penalty for an unsafe release in the pit lane
- Lap 28 --- Scott Dixon and Will Power start moving towards the front as the rest of the field make their pit stops
- Lap 30 --- Joseph Newgarden pits from the lead and comes out in 17th
- Lap 32 --- Colton Herta pits from the lead, allowing Scott Dixon to take the lead
- Lap 33 --- Good battle on track between Alex Palou and Marcus Ericsson
- Lap 41 --- Will Power is having to do a lot of fuel saving
- Lap 51 --- Scott Dixon and Will Power pit, from the lead and second, respectively, allow Josef Newgarden back into the lead
- Lap 58 --- Newgarden pits from the lead and comes back out in 7th, just ahead of Will Power
- At this point Colton Herta is leading
- Lap 61 --- Colton Herta pits from the lead, and comes back out in 3rd, behind Josef Newgarden
- Lap 68 --- Newgarden is catching up to Dixon and challenging him for the lead
- Lap 70 --- Although Newgarden has caught up to Dixon, he doesn't seem to be able to pass him, and Herta is catching up behind him
- Lap 77 --- Newgarden stalls in the hairpin, after contact from behind from Herta allowing Colton Herta and Alex Palou through, dropping into 4th
- /Scott Dixon drove a very tactical race, perfectly balancing going fast and saving fuel/
- /Will Power, who was on approximately the same strategy, finished only 6th/
- /It's a shame that Newgarden was bumped out of contention for the lead, but it was in no way intentional from Herta, so no action was taken/
- /Rosenqvist qualified well, but he was a total non-factor in the race/
